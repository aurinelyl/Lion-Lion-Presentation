Unpretentious Cocktails - These days you might wait two hours for a seat at Death and Co., but a decade ago it was a place you could stop by on a whim early Saturday evenings to have terrific drinks in an attractive, transportive, peaceful space. Lion Lion feels like a throwback to that era. Co-owned by a former bartender at two other of the city’s cocktail temples, Attaboy and Dutch Kills, the basement space is darkly lit by red lampshades; the bar top is black granite; jazz and reggae play over the stereo. In back is a little six-stool bar, plus counter seating opposite; in front are two-seater tables, with retro-feeling, half-moon-shaped red-and-orange banquettes. If you come at happy hour, your bill will be light — drinks like rum swizzles are only $9."
- Grub Street/New York Magazine
